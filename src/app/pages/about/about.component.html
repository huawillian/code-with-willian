Growing Up,

I was surrounded by music. At some point, my weekly schedule would consist of piano, violin, and trumpet lessons as well as orchestra and band practice. And I wasn't always great at it. During a performance, I would play the violin with the up/down motions and without touching the strings. So I practiced frequently and learned that many things take time and patience.

I also learned that perfect practice makes perfect. When starting out with piano, it's important to practice one hand at a time before combining both hands as well as using the correct fingerings to move up and down the scale. Improper practice can lead to bad habits which can prevent one from improving.

I carried these important life lessons throughout my education, career, and personal life.

Competition.
Determination.
Inspiration.

High School

High School was a difficult time of my life. I was suffering from depression and would often indulge in food cravings. However, playing video games helped me overcome this phase.

I played extremely competetive video games with my friends. Every time I lost, I would be even more determined to win. So, I studied the game mechanics, tried innovative strategies, and kept practicing dexterous movements on the controller until I finally won. I found a lot of joy in such friendly competition as we could see how much we improved by continuously challenging each other. I would continue to use it as a way to learn new skills and improve myself.

I also fell in love with the gaming community and how much creativity went into the creation of a game. I eventually started working on my own games even though my school did not teach programming. I remember installing a virtual machine on my family's computer, creating the character pixel sprites, and learning how to program in C++. The result was a pixel graphic zombie survival game with Super Mario like mechanics where you could hide behind trash cans.

Video games inspired me to major in Computer Science and eventually become a software engineer.
